Overview

Zantova Financial Group is a Singapore-headquartered wealth management firm managing $28B AUM across APJC. The firm is midway through a core banking modernisation programme under a hard MAS TRM compliance deadline in Q4 2026. A $12M digital transformation budget is pre-approved and in-cycle for H2 2026, with the CFO directly controlling the mandate.

⚡ Top Pain Points

  • Core banking modernisation stalled by compliance bottlenecks — three internal automation attempts failed in 18 months
  • MAS TRM deadline is non-negotiable; non-compliance risks operating licence review
  • 60-day audit cycles must compress to 7 days — manual process costs $1.8M annually

💡 Opening Angles

  • MAS TRM compliance deadline creates genuine, non-negotiable urgency for Q4 2026
  • Post-acquisition integration of two regional banks creates significant upsell opportunity beyond initial compliance use case
  • CFO has previously approved infrastructure investment — established vendor trust exists

🎯 Strategic Fit

  • API-first architecture aligns with Zantova's core banking modernisation roadmap
  • APJC-based support density meets the mandatory SLA requirements
  • Financial services reference customers in Singapore address procurement risk concern

Technology Priorities

  • Compliance automation and audit workflow compression (60-day → 7-day target)
  • API integration with existing core banking system — zero-customisation preferred
  • Zero-downtime deployment — 24/7 trading operations cannot be interrupted

⚠️ Watch Outs

  • Board sign-off required for deals above $2M — adds 30–45 days to close cycle
  • Korveth Systems has strong brand recognition with the Board; pre-empt reference check before it happens

Overview

Vantelo Logistics is a Singapore-headquartered third-party logistics (3PL) and freight-forwarding provider operating across Southeast Asia, with distribution centres and cross-dock depots in six markets. The firm is breaching fulfilment SLAs during peak season because patchy network visibility across warehouses, ports and its delivery fleet creates shipment-tracking blind spots and slow incident response. A new COO with a digital-supply-chain mandate joined eight weeks ago, creating both significant risk and a narrow window of opportunity.

⚡ Top Pain Points

  • Network visibility gaps across depots, ports and the delivery fleet causing manual exception triage and SLA breaches
  • Lost 3 enterprise shipper contracts in 12 months — 12-hour average incident resolution across warehouse sites is the root cause
  • Unplanned WMS/TMS downtime costs $380K annually — board-level concern with active customer-churn risk

💡 Opening Angles

  • $380K annual downtime cost is a board-visible metric — ROI case is pre-built
  • New COO is 8 weeks in and still forming vendor opinions — early engagement is critical
  • Existing SAP and WMS/TMS environment creates a natural integration wedge

🎯 Strategic Fit

  • Network visibility capabilities directly address the multi-site depot, port and fleet connectivity gap
  • 24/7 APAC support model meets mandatory peak-season SLA requirements across Southeast Asia operations
  • TCO advantage vs hyperscaler-native tooling — especially on APAC support quality for distributed physical sites — is the primary differentiation

Technology Priorities

  • Real-time network visibility across distribution centres, port links and the delivery fleet
  • Integration with SAP and WMS/TMS for automated exception triage and escalation
  • APAC 24/7 support with sub-30-minute escalation SLA for warehouse and depot incidents

⚠️ Watch Outs

  • New COO came from a cloud-first background — likely has established relationships with a hyperscaler-native networking stack
  • Internal build vs buy debate ongoing — no decision to engage external network vendors yet
  • Rachel Wong (champion) has limited executive influence — deal cannot progress without COO engagement
